FAQ

Educators, students, and procurement — including questions we hear in school procurement conversations.

Who is FairShare for?
Higher education, sixth form / college, and high school group work where you want clearer coordination and fairer visibility into contributions — without replacing your LMS overnight.
Does FairShare replace our LMS?
No. FairShare focuses on group project execution — tasks, docs, chat, and contribution signals. Most institutions keep Moodle, Canvas, Blackboard, or Google Classroom for modules, submission, and grades.
How do you handle UK GDPR?
We document our role (often processor to your institution), list subprocessors, support Data Processing Agreements, and describe retention and security on our Security & trust page. Your DPO validates fit for your school.
What about PDPL and cross-border transfers?
Institutions should assess transfers (e.g. UK/EU hosting or AI subprocessors) under applicable PDPL rules. We provide information for your transfer analysis; legal sign-off sits with your organisation.
Is student data safe?
Run FairShare on a properly configured host, use strong authentication, and follow our security documentation. We do not market FairShare as a consumer toy — it is intended for institutional use with appropriate governance.
What does “contribution analytics” mean?
Signals from activity and tasks — useful for coaching and spotting imbalance. They are not proof of misconduct; instructors remain responsible for academic judgment.
SSO and school domains?
Campus-tier customers can discuss SSO and domain allowlists as part of procurement. Roadmap items are confirmed in writing — not promised on this marketing page alone.
Can we export data if we leave?
Institutional contracts should define export and deletion windows. The product includes export paths for key artefacts; confirm scope with sales for Campus agreements.
How do we get started?
Book a demo, run a Starter pilot with one cohort, then move to Team or Campus when procurement and safeguarding sign-off are in place.
